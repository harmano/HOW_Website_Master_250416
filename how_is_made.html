How Vinyl Film is Made

The manufacturing process of vinyl films directly impacts their suitability for various applications, durability, and cost. The two main methods of vinyl production are casting and calendering, each offering different characteristics and performance.

In General.

Vinyl, or Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC), is primarily derived from two key materials: salt and crude oil. The production process begins with the extraction of chlorine from salt using electrolytic dissociation. Crude oil or natural gas is processed through a method called cracking, which breaks down the petroleum into ethylene, the base component used to manufacture vinyl.

The Types.

Manufacturing determines their suitability for various applications, their durability, and their cost. There are currently two primary forms of mass production of vinyl films and they are casting and calendaring.

(1) Calendered Film: This method involves extruding the vinyl mixture through heated rollers. The materials are kneaded and pressed into thin sheets, resulting in a thicker, stiffer film that is easier to handle but less flexible than cast vinyl.

Benefits
  • - Stiffer and thicker for easier handling
  • - More economical than cast film
  • - Calendered films have a higher resistance to abrasion
Recommended Uses for Calendered Vinyl
  • - Sun Shade Visor
  • - Floor Graphics
  • - Pre Spaced Lettering
  • - Window Decals
  • - Wall Art
  • - Labels

(2) Cast Film: Cast vinyl is created by pouring a liquid mixture (known as organosol) onto a casting sheet and processing it through a series of ovens. The resulting film is thinner, more flexible, and conforms well to complex shapes and curves.

Benefits
  • - Due the production method used, cast films don't tend to shrink with age
  • - Extended life and durability due to the quality of plasticizers and raw materials
  • - Available in much thinner gauges so they conform to substrates with corrugations and complex curves.
  • - Maintenance of color and other properties better than calendered film
  • - Less energy intensive process used means it's cost-effective to do small production runs of special colors giving a wider range of colors to you.
Recommended Uses for Cast Vinyl
  • - Vehicle wraps
  • - Architectural wraps
  • - Smartphone (iPhone, Android) wraps

In Summary.

The manufacturing process for vinyl films is constantly evolving, with manufacturers innovating through proprietary methods and patents. This allows for tailored solutions depending on the specific use case, whether for architectural applications, vehicle wraps, or graphics. The continuous advancements make vinyl films highly versatile, but also explain why there is no “perfect” vinyl film—each application may require a different blend of performance characteristics.

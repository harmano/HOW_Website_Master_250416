Applications of Vinyl

Manufacturing and Applications of Vinyl Films Manufacturing determines the suitability of vinyl films for various applications, their durability, and their cost. There are two primary forms of mass production: casting and calendaring.

1. Doors.

Architectural vinyl films can mimic real wood, stone, metal, and marble finishes. These films are ideal for both pre-construction and renovations, providing a faster, cleaner alternative to traditional methods. Vinyl can be applied to any door surface, creating a fresh, modern appearance.

- Benefits: Greater durability, flexibility in design, and easy post-installation corrections.

1. Columns.

Vinyl films can completely transform interior or exterior columns by mimicking real materials such as marble or metal. This provides an affordable, durable way to elevate the aesthetic of any space.

1. Millwork.

Architectural vinyl films offer a cost-effective and versatile option for covering millwork, from trim to intricate woodwork. These films can be applied without damaging the underlying material, providing a clean, updated look.

1. Cabinets.

Vinyl films are an excellent choice for cabinet refurbishments, offering a variety of textures and finishes that mimic real materials. The quick application process allows for minimal disruption, giving cabinets a fresh, new look at a fraction of the cost.

1. Countertops.

Renovate countertops without the hassle of replacement by applying architectural vinyl films. With heat-resistant and durable finishes, vinyl offers a high-end look without the associated cost.

1. Baseboards.

Baseboards can be easily covered with vinyl films, providing protection from scuffs and wear while adding a touch of style. Whether you want to mimic wood, stone, or metal, vinyl provides the perfect solution for an easy upgrade.

1. Tables.

Vinyl films can be applied to tables, making them look like high-end materials such as wood, stone, or metal. They are a practical solution for updating furniture at a fraction of the cost of purchasing new items.

- Benefits: Faster application and lower costs compared to purchasing new tables or refinishing existing surfaces with traditional materials.

1. Backsplashes.

Architectural vinyl films are ideal for creating a custom backsplash in kitchens and bathrooms. These films provide a waterproof, heat-resistant, and stylish finish, without the need for expensive and labor-intensive tile installation.

- Benefits: Easier installation, cost savings, and more design flexibility compared to traditional tile or stone backsplashes.

2. Walls / Panels.

Vinyl films can conform to flat or curved surfaces, making them perfect for walls, panels, and ceilings. With unlimited patterns to choose from, you can recreate the look of real materials like wood, stone, or metal. Vinyl saves time and money in both construction and renovation projects.

Benefits: Easy to install, durable, and cost-effective, vinyl can last up to 20 years, depending on type and conditions. Compared to traditional materials, vinyl films allow for faster installations with minimal disruption and cost.

3. Front Desk / Lobby.

Easy to install and conform to a variety of flat or curved surfaces including walls, panels, and ceilings. With more than 1,000 patterns to choose from, clients can recreate the look of real wood finishes, stone, metal, marble and more.

- Benfits Wall wraps and decals are a great way to brand an office with company logos, colours, and other sayings or brand references. Visual branding in an office space or work station is a successful way to build culture, employee pride and brand image.

4. Elevator.

Elevators are frequently renovated with architectural vinyl films due to their durability and design flexibility. These films stand up well to scratches and other damage, making them perfect for high-traffic areas like hotel lobbies and office buildings.

- Benfits Quick installation, easy to maintain, and capable of lasting over ten years, depending on the type and care.

Why Vinyl is Better than Traditional Methods:

Architectural vinyl films offer significant advantages over traditional renovation materials such as wood, stone, or metal. Vinyl films are more affordable, quicker to install, and require less maintenance. Unlike traditional materials that may require heavy tools, adhesives, and long installation times, vinyl film installations are clean, silent, and fast, reducing downtime and labor costs. Vinyl’s versatility also allows it to be applied to a wider range of surfaces, including curved and textured areas, where traditional materials may be more difficult and expensive to work with. Additionally, the durability and design flexibility of vinyl make it a long-lasting, customizable solution for modern renovations.

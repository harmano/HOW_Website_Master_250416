Types of Popular Vinyl Finishes.

Transform most smooth non-porous surface with one of our 1300 architectural vinyl film patterns, including natural wood, stone, marble, metals, leathers, fabrics, and many others. The multilayer film structure completely imitates patterns and textures of real finishes. Architectural film is self-adhering, can be installed on most smooth flat and curved surfaces (walls, ceilings, elevator cabs, doors, furniture, etc.), indoors and outdoors, and has a proven track record of durability.

Architectural Film Longevity

Depending on the conditions and surface applied to and the vinyl type, properly installed vinyl film can maintain its look for 20 years, almost double that of paint.

Architectural Film Explained

Architectural vinyl film is designed for surface finishes. It replicates the look of natural materials like wood, stone, metal, and fabric, while providing a flexible, durable, and lightweight alternative for interior and exterior applications.

READ MORE

What is Architectural Film?

Vinyl film is a versatile plastic that is a thin laminate film that can be installed to the interior or exterior of most surfaces in homes and buildings. Largely used for automobiles, Architectural Vinyl Film is beginning to gain main stream traction.
